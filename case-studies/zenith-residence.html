Zenith Residence

Luxury Residential • Malibu, California • 2024

Project Overview

A contemporary masterpiece overlooking the Pacific Ocean

Zenith Residence represents the pinnacle of contemporary coastal living, seamlessly integrating sustainable design principles with luxury amenities. Positioned on a dramatic bluff overlooking the Pacific Ocean, this 5,000 square foot residence challenges conventional notions of beachfront architecture.

The project required innovative solutions to address challenging site conditions, strict coastal regulations, and the client's vision for a net-zero energy home that maximizes both privacy and panoramic views.

Client Challenge

Balancing luxury, sustainability, and coastal regulations

Our client, a technology entrepreneur, sought to create a net-zero energy residence that would serve as both a primary residence and an entertaining space for business associates. The challenge was multifaceted: strict coastal development regulations limited building height and setbacks, while the client's sustainability goals required innovative energy systems.

Additionally, the site's exposure to salt air, seismic activity, and extreme weather conditions demanded materials and construction methods that could withstand coastal environmental stresses while maintaining the aesthetic integrity of the design.

Our Solution

Innovative design strategies for coastal luxury living

Terraced Design

Multi-level terraced structure that follows the natural slope while maximizing ocean views from every level.

Sustainable Systems

Integrated solar panels, geothermal heating, and rainwater collection systems achieve net-zero energy consumption.

Resilient Materials

Marine-grade steel, weathering concrete, and impact-resistant glazing ensure longevity in harsh coastal conditions.

Project Results

Measurable outcomes and client satisfaction

Zenith Residence achieved LEED Platinum certification and exceeded the client's net-zero energy goals, generating 15% more energy than it consumes annually. The innovative terraced design preserved 80% of the site's natural vegetation while creating 3,000 square feet of outdoor living space.

The project was completed on schedule and within budget, receiving recognition from the American Institute of Architects and California Coastal Commission for its exemplary integration of sustainability and design excellence.
